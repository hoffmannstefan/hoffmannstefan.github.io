I am a researcher in theoretical computer science and previously worked as a data scientist and full stack developer at a psychiatric hospital, managing patient and study data, and implementing and maintaining a software system to access these data. In my research, I contributed to the following fields of study:
  • automata theory, more specifically problems of synchronization and state complexity
  • formal language theory, in particular commutative languages and the shuffle operation
  • topological investigations on omega languages
I also offered courses and seminars on the topics of my research, as well as on the mathematical foundations of computer science and parameterized complexity during my time at Trier University.

My Erdös number is three (via Henning Fernau or Ludwig Staiger, both two).

Research interest keywords: automata, formal language theory, synchronization, (finite) permutation group theory, permutation automata, group languages, parameterized complexity, computational complexity, shuffle product, partial commutativity, commutative languages, state complexity, omega languages.
